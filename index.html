About

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Forest and Wildlife Ecology at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, advised by Prof. Min Chen in the Global Change Research Laboratory. I received my bachelor's degree in Spatial Information and Digital Technology from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China.

My research interests include, but are not limited to, forestry applications of LiDAR, the interaction between human activities and climate change, and human adaptation to climate change.

Research interests

A non‑exhaustive list of topics my work has touched on:

  • · LiDAR for forest structure — terrestrial, drone, and airborne laser scanning; tree extraction, LAI, gap fraction, canopy top height, 3D canopy structure, point‑cloud registration
  • · Spaceborne remote sensing of vegetation — PRISMA, NEON AOP, CYGNSS, Sentinel‑1
  • · Plant functional traits — geospatial gradients of leaf and canopy traits and their evolutionary strategies; trait retrieval from leaf spectroscopy using transfer learning
  • · Forest edges — global and regional mapping, dynamics, disturbance drivers, ecosystem consequences
  • · Forest hydrology — evapotranspiration, rainfall interception, canopy‑structure controls on water fluxes
  • · Land surface modeling — CLM5 and ELM/E3SM
  • · Vegetation dynamics in extreme environments — Antarctic greening and its drivers; seasonal wetland mapping
  • · Biodiversity and mammal migration — patterns of mammal movement and the role of wildlife in climate mitigation
  • · Human–environment interactions — how human activities drive land‑use and land‑cover change (LULCC); human adaptation to climate change
